Potential factors associated with COVID-19 spread and mortality rate

The risk of spread and mortality of a disease is mainly associated with the general health of the population, but also multiple other factors such as age, country policies, air pollution, climate, etc.... COVID-19, declared as a global pandemic by the WHO on the 11th March 2020, has rapidly and widely spread around the world, infecting over 711,000 people and leaving over 38,769 deaths within three months. Based on the political, social, economic, cultural, geographical resemblances of the MENA region's countries, we analysed the current recorded statistics of COVID-19 to identify potential risk factors associated with COVID-19’s spread and mortality in the context of MENA region countries. We used COIVD-19 data to measure covariance between different development indicators in order to deduce correlated variables. The number of tuberculosis’s incidences and the percentage of the population living in rural areas have been identified as potential mortality factors, high literacy rates and high GDP were identified as potential recovery factors. Countries with a high percentage of the population living in urban areas or older populations recorded a higher number of confirmed cases.

Detection of 2019 novel coronavirus using Ultrasound images and deep Learning

In the context of the Corona Virus 2019 pandemic and given the lack of sensitivity on initial RT-PCR testing described by multiple studies, the use of imaging procedures (especially CT scan and X-ray) to detect and monitor Covid-19 patients has shown his accuracy in identifying abnormalities manifesting caused by Covid-19 pneumonia and are to date used combined with PCR as the main tools for the current COVID-19 detection. However, considering the highly propagation of the virus and the increasing number of confirmed cases and also the health care structures especially in morocco, the availability of those machines is not guaranteed for all the Covid-19 patients hence the utility of using Ultrasound as a detecting and monitoring tool. In fact, recent studies have proved the utility of using LUNG ULTRASOUND as an alternative of CT scans during this pandemic to manage Covid-19 with respiratory involvement. The aim of this project is to apply advanced deep learning methods to detect Covid-19 based on Ultrasound manifestations of coronavirus pneumonia using multiple models. The implementation of this system in the Moroccan hospitals will help health workers and assist them in the process of pre-sort severe cases and in the monitoring of lung region affected by the virus and his treatment response. In addition to that, it will reduce the risk of contamination and restrict the utilisation of CT scan for the critical cases or those where the US doesn’t give an accurate diagnosis.

COVID-19 Detection using X-ray / CT scan images and Deep Convolutional Neural Networks.

The 2019 novel coronavirus (COVID-19), which starts in China, has spread rapidly among people living in other countries and has become a pandemic. Therefore, it is necessary to set up an automatic detection system as a quick alternative diagnostic option to prevent the spread of COVID-19 among people. Our goal is to detect COVID-19 using X-rays, computed tomography images and deep convolutional neural networks. In our study, we proposed an automatic prediction of COVID-19 using a deep convolutional neural network based on pre-trained transfer models (VGG16, ResNet50, MobileNetV2, MobileNetV2) for chest radiographic images and CT scans, We plan to program a graphical interface that can provide COVID / Non-Covid detection based on the models implemented.

Development of epidemiologic compartmental models of COVID-19 in Morocco.

The World Health Organization declared Covid-19 a pandemic on March 11, 2020. At the time of writing, the number of confirmed cases worldwide quickly exceeded half a million, highlighting the long-term risk of further global spread. It is therefore important to know what to expect in terms of the growth in the number of cases, and to understand what is needed to stop the very worrying trends. Morocco registered its first case of covid-19 infection on 2 March 2020, since that day, the epidemic has affected all regions of the country, with a total number of cases that exceeded 3000. The aim of this project is to develop an epidemiological model from mathematical equations, statistical functions or computer programs. These models can incorporate covid-19 characteristics (contagiousness, virulence), modes of transmission (direct, indirect, vector), population vulnerability factors, mobility, barrier or preventive measures to limit exposure. Our approach aims to follow possible scenarios of the spread of the virus within Morocco.
